Beam for Android TV

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 20 July 2026

The short version

Beam collects zero data. No analytics, no tracking, no telemetry, no crash reports, no account system, no sign-up — nothing leaves your device.

What the app stores

Beam stores library metadata, watch history, playback position, and cached artwork locally on your Android TV device. This data never leaves the device and is deleted when you uninstall the app.

Network connections

The only network requests Beam makes are the ones you initiate:

  • Streaming from your Mega account or seedbox
  • Fetching poster artwork and metadata from TMDB and OMDb
  • Serving the local web dashboard to devices on your own network

There are no background pings, no phone-home endpoints, and no third-party SDKs embedded in the app.

Payments

Purchases are handled entirely by Stripe on their hosted checkout page. Beam never sees, processes, or stores your payment details. Stripe's own privacy policy applies to that transaction: stripe.com/privacy.

Children

Beam does not collect data from anyone, including children. Kids Mode is a local UI feature with no network component.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new date. Given that the policy is "we collect nothing," changes are unlikely.

Contact

Questions? Reply to your Stripe receipt email or visit the Beam site.
